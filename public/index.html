Dan Bjorge

I'm VP of Product Architecture at Deque Systems, where we build tools to help make software more accessible to folks with disabilities. I coordinate engineering efforts and standards across the company's product division, and I help maintain axe-core, an open source accessibility testing engine that's been downloaded billions of times. I also contribute to the W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) standard, mostly focused on maintaining WCAG 2.

Before Deque, I spent over a decade at Microsoft. I most recently worked on Accessibility Insights, an open-source toolkit that helps developers find and fix accessibility issues in their software. I've also worked on the Windows Engineering System developing build and test infrastructure, as well several different layers of the Windows networking stack.

Outside of work, some of the projects I'm proudest of include leading nearly 100 contributors in the development of a community Slay the Spire mod, designing and building the activity tracking system for Seattle Humane's Dog Behavior and Socialization volunteers, and self-publishing an Android app for detecting misuse of push notifications for ads that reached 6 million users before it was sunset (Android eventually built it into the OS).

You can find me on GitHub and LinkedIn.
